About Us

Our history, achievements and future plans — Department of Computing and Informatics

Our History

Bugema University was founded in 1948 by the Seventh-day Adventist Church as a training institution. Over the decades it grew into a fully chartered university, becoming one of Uganda's most respected institutions of higher learning.

The Department of Computing and Informatics was established to meet the growing demand for technology professionals in Uganda and the East African region. Since its inception, the department has trained thousands of graduates who now serve in government, private sector, NGOs, and international organisations.

Our Mission

To provide high-quality, accessible computing education that fosters innovation, critical thinking, and ethical practice — preparing graduates for successful careers and meaningful contributions to society.

Our Vision

To be a leading center of excellence in computing and informatics education in East Africa, producing graduates who are innovative, ethical, and globally competitive in the technology industry.

Our Achievements

  • Over 1,000 graduates placed in technology roles across Uganda and East Africa
  • NCHE-accredited programmes recognised across the region
  • State-of-the-art computer labs with 60+ modern workstations
  • Successful annual Tech Symposium attracting industry leaders
  • Memoranda of understanding signed with leading technology companies
  • Launch of the Bachelor of Science in Applied Data Science and AI in 2026
  • Consistent production of first-class graduates in computing disciplines
